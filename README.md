# 🚌 Abuja Public Transport Route Planning Research

> **Business Question:** How can Abuja's Federal Capital Territory (FCT) develop an efficient, data-driven public transport network that addresses the city's rapid population growth, traffic congestion, and current service gaps?

---

## 🗂 Project Overview

A comprehensive **transport route planning research report** prepared for Peramare Transport Company Ltd, covering primary survey data, Origin-Destination (O-D) analysis, GIS-informed route mapping, and a phased implementation strategy for Abuja's Federal Capital Territory. The research combines qualitative and quantitative methods to deliver actionable recommendations for a multi-modal public transport network serving 400+ buses and 2,000+ taxis.

## 🔍 Research Methodology

### 1. Primary Data Collection
Structured questionnaires were administered across **10 high-demand districts** in the FCT using cluster sampling:

| District | Key Finding |
|---|---|
| Berger | 60% male users, 40% earning ₦150,001+ |
| Maitama | 80% male users, highest private car dependence |
| Airport Junction | 67% aged 6-18 years (school trip dominated) |
| Wuse | 60% civil servants, 80% aged 19-40 |
| Gwarimpa | Mixed demographic, 67% married |

### 2. O-D Analysis — 2004 vs 2024 Projections

Traffic count projections across 25 FCT locations revealed dramatic growth:

| Route | 2024 Projected Count | Share |
|---|---|---|
| Airport Road (by Games Village) | 140,349 | 41.4% |
| Keffi Road (AYA–Nyanya) | 112,607 | 33.2% |
| Kubwa Road (Tipper Garage) | 86,366 | 25.5% |
| **Total** | **339,322** | **100%** |

**Modal Split Finding:** Cars dominate at **74%** of all trips vs. 14% small buses and 8% medium buses — confirming the urgent need for high-capacity public transport.

### 3. Demand Pattern Analysis
E-hailing surge maps were observed over multiple days to identify:
- **Morning rush:** High demand in residential areas (Gwarimpa, Jabi, Mabushi)
- **Evening rush:** High demand in the Central Business District

## 💡 Key Findings

| Finding | Insight |
|---|---|
| **Cars dominate (74% modal share)** | Urgent need for quality public transport to shift modal split |
| **64% dissatisfied with punctuality** | Reliability is the #1 complaint among current public transport users |
| **57% use public transport daily** | Strong latent demand exists — satisfaction is the barrier, not usage |
| **87% have no access to online route info** | Digital integration is a major gap in current service |
| **Airport Road is busiest axis (41%)** | Highest bus frequency should be prioritised on this corridor |

## 🗺 Route Design

### High-Capacity Bus Network
Based on analysis, **7 bus routes** were designed covering:
- Gwagwalada – Eagle Square (adjusted via Constitution Avenue)
- Kubwa – City Centre
- Madalla – City Centre
- Masaka – City Centre
- Building Market – Area 3
- Gwarimpa – Gude Market
- Ring Road 2 (Katampe Extension – Apo Roundabout) — **new route**

### Taxi Pool System
**5 taxi pool routes** mapped across the FCC to complement bus routes and cover gaps where high-capacity buses cannot operate efficiently.

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations

1. **Prioritise Airport Road corridor** — highest traffic volume (41%) warrants maximum bus frequency and dedicated bus lanes
2. **Launch digital route information** — 87% of users have no online access to routes; a simple mobile-friendly route map would dramatically improve user experience
3. **Address reliability first** — 64% dissatisfied with punctuality; GPS tracking and real-time updates should be integrated from Phase I
4. **Replace Berger–Eagle Square bus route with Taxi Pool** — more efficient for that corridor's demand pattern
5. **Deploy electric taxis within FCC** — mitigates bus network gaps while supporting environmental targets
